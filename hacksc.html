What is a Code-A-thon

Our Code-A-Thon, often called Hackathons, is where a group of passionate developers and designers create something amazing in a limited time frame. This can range from websites, to iOS apps, to even Oculus Rift Games. We are giving students like you an opportunity to form learn something new, make something new, and showcase your product! It's always a ton of fun and we have so many people here to make your visions come true!

HackSC: High School Edition

This particular Code-A-Thon is a great way for high schoolers like yourself to get interested in what Computer Science can do. Our event will be from 10 am - 8 pm on USC's campus with lunch and dinner included. There will be many mentors around to help you guys with your products but you will be the one leading the project. We are really excited to see what you build!

FAQ

Do I need any experience?

Absolutely Not! In fact we encourage those that have little to no experience in Computer Science to apply. This codeathon is meant as a learning processes and we will definitely be teaching you a lot. You will be surprised at how much you can actually accomplish with just a little bit of our tools.

Who can participate?

Currently we will be asking only students are in 9th-12th grade and attend 32nd Street School to apply as we do not have that much accomadations for everyone. However in the future, we definitely plan on increasing the number of people that can apply.

How much will it cost?

Absolutely Nothing. We just ask you guys come down on a Saturday Morning at around 9:30 and stay with us till around 8 PM. Food and Swag will be provided, we just ask you guys to make cool things!

What do I need to bring?

Nothing! Again we will be providing food and snacks, space for you guys to work, and laptops to work on. However you are welcome to bring your own laptops and any software that you think would be useful.

Are there teams or am I working alone?

We definitely encourage everyone to work in a team as that is how the real world is structured. Plus it is generally more fun to work with your friends and bounce ideas of one another. We recommend trying to with a team of 3-4. We will be helping you guys set up a team coding enviorment so you guys can make a cool website/app together.

Will there be prizes?

Although the main point of this event is for you guys to learn what you can do in Computer Science, we will also be judging the best apps and giving out small monetary prizes. However the biggest prize we could possibly give is the ability to learn a new skill.
